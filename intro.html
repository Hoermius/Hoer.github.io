Sense of place.

The term sense of place has been used many ways in which knowing the place is a recognizing how people use different places to fulfill the practical needs of living. The concept of sense of place describes our relationship with places, expressed in the different perspectives of human life such as sociocultural, emotions, and personal experience. The aspect of the relationships to place consists the type of bond and process which focus ideological and spiritual relationship in order to choose "environment" to reflect my personal experience.

In Ideological Relationship

The ideological relationships are founded on conscious values and beliefs about how humans should relate to physical places. The defining characteristic of ideological relationships is a well-articulated ideology about how to live in a place. "Urban City" is my ideological place to reflect my cultural background.

In Spiritual Relationship

Spiritual relationship is feeling a sense of belonging which seem to be more of an intuitive connection than an emotional, cognitive, or material connection. The aspect of relationships to places based on something much less tangible than personal history. Knowledge is the primary value that what I focus on my life. "Libary" can be the spiritual place that reflects how I live.
